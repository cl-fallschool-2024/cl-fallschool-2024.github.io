About Passau

This year's CL Fall School is located at the University of Passau, situated in the heart of Bavaria.

The city of Passau is situated at the confluence of three rivers - the Danube, the Inn, and the Ilz - and is therefore often referred to as the “City on Three Rivers”. Passau is characterized by its historic builidings, narrow cobblestone streets and inviting squares that invite for leisurely strolls. The St. Stephen’s cathedral and the Veste Oberhaus are only two of Passau’s impressive architectural gems.

If you are not into architecture and history, Passau also offers a vibrant cultural scene. You can enjoy local cuisine and the famous Bavarian beers in the numerous beer gardens in the city.

University of Passau

The University of Passau is situated in a picturesque setting close to the city’s historic Old Town. Approximately 11,000 students benefit from the diverse academic offerings, as the university is known for its excellence in teaching and research across a wide range of disciplines, from humanities and social sciences to natural sciences and engineering.

How to get to Passau

If you are travelling by plane, the closest airports to Passau are Munich Airport (MUC) and Linz Airport (LNZ) in Austria. To come to Passau you have the option to take an (international) long-distance train or drive to Passau by car.

You can also reach Passau via the airport Nuremberg (NUE) and Vienna (VIE), although railway ticket costs are expected to be higher.

For information on railway connections to Passau, please visit the Deutsche Bahn website.
